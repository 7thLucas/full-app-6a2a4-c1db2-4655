Working title: **Pipeline** (placeholder personal CRM; branding not finalized).

## What it is
A simple, personal CRM for a solo operator (founder, freelancer, consultant, salesperson) who is both salesperson and account manager. It tracks contacts, logs every interaction, and shows an at-a-glance sales pipeline. Deliberately lightweight — none of the weight of an enterprise CRM.

## Who it's for
The hands-on relationship-owner: one person tracking contacts in their head, a spreadsheet, or scattered notes, who loses deals to forgotten follow-ups rather than lost pitches.

## The problem
Deals slip silently. Follow-ups are forgotten, "last touch" is invisible, and there's no single place showing which relationships are going cold. The cost is lost revenue from contacts never followed up.

## The solution — three connected pieces
1. **Contacts** — people with name, company, and an automatically maintained "last touch" date.
2. **Interaction log** — timestamped calls, emails, and notes per contact; logging an interaction updates that contact's "last touch."
3. **Pipeline** — a stage board where each contact/deal moves through Lead → Contacted → Proposal → Won / Lost. These are the default stages and are renamable.

The three are linked: an interaction updates last-touch, and the pipeline always reflects current reality.

## Core principle
Make the cold ones impossible to miss. The product's job is to surface who has gone quiet and needs a follow-up, so revenue stops leaking through forgotten contacts. Cold-contact surfacing is the hero feature, not an afterthought.

## Tone
Calm, focused, trustworthy. Speaks to a busy operator who wants clarity, not clutter. No jargon, no enterprise bloat.

## Scope (current)
- In: contacts, interaction logging, pipeline stages, last-touch / cold-contact surfacing.
- Out (for now): team/multi-user collaboration, email integration/sync, automation, reporting dashboards beyond the pipeline view.
